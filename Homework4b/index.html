(HW4b) Active Fire Data: Currently Burning Fires

The data in the table represents fire detection in the contiguous US states and Hawaii over a 7 day period.

Confidence represents the likelihood that a detected hotspot is actually a fire and not indirect heat sources, where higher values indicate a higher probability that the detected thermal anomaly is indeed a fire. Brightness represents the temperature of the fire in Kelvin (K) as detected by a satelite's thermal infrared sensor. The higher the brightness the more intense the fire.

Red = Low Confidence, Yellow = Medium Confidence, Green = High Confidence
